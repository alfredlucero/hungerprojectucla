UCLA Hunger Project is an undergraduate-run service organization that aims to combat homelessness while raising awareness in the Greater Los Angeles area. Our mission is to not only provide basic aid but to also help disadvantaged members reintegrate into society. We are a community of volunteers, interns, and student leaders dedicated towards our cause. Throughout the year, we work with local non-profit organizations such as People Assisting the Homeless (PATH) and The Westwood Transitional House Village of the Salvation Army in order to provide to our service recipients mentoring, food, and educational services.
