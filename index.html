HUNGER PROJECT
UCLA

﻿Founded in 1987, Hunger Project is dedicated to fighting poverty by combining direct service, advocacy, and education. Volunteers deliver food, serve meals, provide job assistance, and teach life skills classes. Our ultimate goal is to provide services to homeless and low-income individuals in helping meet their immediate and long-term needs. Hunger Project at UCLA has always implemented programs to alleviate some of the problems of homelessness in Los Angeles. We do this by informing and educating UCLA students and leading by example through volunteering programs. At the same time we encourage students to become active in the community by offering volunteer programs that are accessible to anyone in the UCLA community. The two active programs that have persisted throughout the history of Hunger Project at UCLA are the food salvage program and the health clinic program.

WESTWOOD TRANSITIONAL VILLAGE

WHAT:

Westwood Transitional Village (WTV) is a residential temporary housing facility funded by the Salvation Army. The units house around 150 residents, many being the families of veterans, and with two thirds of the population being children.

INVOLVEMENT:

At WTV, we reach out to the children living at the village. Most of our events focus on fun games that will get the kids involved and running around. In addition, we incorporate important life lessons that will teach the children how to live impactful and fulfilling lives.

WHERE:

Westwood Transitional Village -1401 S. Sepulveda Boulevard, Los Angeles, CA 90025

PEOPLE ASSISTING THE HOMELESS

WHAT:

PATH is a nonprofit which provides temporary housing to residents while they get back on their feet. They have multiple housing locations around the LA area and also provide supportive services to help people get off the streets and into a permanent housing solution.

INVOLVEMENT:

We provide support to the residents by cooking delicious and nutritious food for them. Nutrition is a big focus for this program, as it can be difficult to get a balanced diet when funds are limited. Following the food preparation and serving, we also share a meal with the residents to get to know them on a personal level.

WHERE:

PATH West LA - 2346 Cotner Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90064
PATH Hollywood - 5627 Fernwood Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90028

HOMELESS AWARENESS WEEK

WHAT:

Homeless Awareness Week is a collaborative event hosted by the various UCLA organizations dealing with homelessness in the community. It takes place once a year, and is intended to bring greater awareness of the problems that come with homelessness to UCLA’s communities.

INVOLVEMENT:

Hunger Project plays a large role in providing volunteers and leading discussions for events throughout the week. While tabling for the event, UCLA students are encouraged to come to the Hunger Project table and help prepare hygiene kits and snack packages that we will distribute to recipients on Skid Row. Hunger Banquet is also hosted during this week.

WHERE:

UCLA

HUNGER BANQUET

WHAT:

Taking place during Homeless Awareness Week, Hunger Banquet is another way for us to show UCLA students what homelessness in the LA community is like. As part of this program, students are randomly given ‘socioeconomic standings’ that dictate what kinds of foods they will have access to, to show the kinds of limitations that poverty places upon those who undergo it. In addition the event highlights the work and experiences of community leaders that work with the homeless.

INVOLVEMENT:

We organize and run it!

WHERE:

UCLA

MIDNIGHT MISSION

WHAT:

The Midnight Mission is a highly organized nonprofit that serves the residents of Skid Row by serving free meals every day of the year. They also offer transitional services that connect residents with necessary resources.

INVOLVEMENT:

We volunteer with the Midnight Mission to help cook and serve food to residents at Skid Row.

WHERE:

Skid Row 601 South San Pedro Street, Los Angeles, CA 90014

SKID ROW DISTRIBUTIONS

WHAT:

A raw service experience, distributing supplies to the residents of Skid Row on foot. Eye-opening and highly rewarding, we pack and distribute food and hygiene kits to residents during the afternoon.

INVOLVEMENT:

We organize and run it!

WHERE:

Skid Row
